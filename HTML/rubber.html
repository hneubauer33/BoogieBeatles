Rubber Soul

September 3, 1965

“I liked the way we got our faces to be longer on the album cover. We lost the ‘little innocents’ tag, the naivety, and Rubber Soul was the first one where we were fully-fledged potheads.” - George Harrison

Rubber soul pushed the boundaries of Beatles Songs and started exploring larger themes. “Nowhere Man” touches on Lennon’s lack of confidence while “I’m looking through you” dives into McCartney’s often turbulent relationship with Jane Asher. We can especially see Lennon’s song writing skills in “Norweigan Wood” which is also the first western pop song to use the sitar. “In Drive My Car” Harrison was inspired by Otis Redding for the guitar and bass. The stretch effect seen on the album cover was an accident after they had chosen the photograph taken in Lennon’s backyard.
