With The Beatles

November 22, 1963

“We could play different stuff live from what was recorded and still have stuff left over for the next album” - Paul Mccartney

The Beatle’s second studio album features 14 new tracks including George Harrisons’ first song “Don’t Bother Me”. The most complicated song was “Money (That’s What I Want)” because of the number of overdubs. Covers included Chuck Berry’s “Roll over Beethoven” and The Donay’s “Devil in Her Heart. The focus on R&B and Moton demonstrated the continuous maturity of the Band. The songs touched on themes such as love, loss, and money. Appealing to a wider audience, spending a total of 51 weeks on the top 20. The artwork was taken by photographer Robert Freedman at the Palace Court Hotel, Bournemouth, while The Beatles were on tour. The album was the first British long player ever to sell more than one million copies.
