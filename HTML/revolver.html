Revolver

August 5, 1966

“We were really starting to find ourselves in the studio. We were finding what we could do, just being the four of us and playing our instruments. The overdubbing got better, even though it was always pretty tricky because of the lack of tracks. The songs got more interesting, so with that the effects got more interesting.” - Ringo Starr

Revolver is often considered the Beatles best body of work, saying goodbye to the old Beatles and hello to an experimental group working together. The band had decided to stop touring leaving them amble time to explore in the studio. The album features orchestral instrumentation, backward recording and inspiration from indian music and childrens songs. The most innovative track on the album is “tomorrow never knows” which was an adaption of the book Tibetan Book of the Dead.
