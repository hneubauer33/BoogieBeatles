Please Please Me

March 22, 1963

“Oh god this is it, a piece of plastic. And this piece of plastic was like gold, you know. You’d sell your soul. You would sell your soul to get on that little record.” - Ringo Starr

Following the success of their number 1 hit single Please Please Me, the album was rushed released to the UK. Produced by George Martin, the band recorded the entirety of the album in 13 hours. George Martin ensured that they would get the best sound which is seen throughout the album. The band saved Twist and Shout to be recorded last in order to save John Lennon’s voice for the rest of the songs. The album name was inspired by Bing Crosby’s tune “Please.” Lennon was intrigued in the double use of the word please in the lyrics. In this first album the Beatles were moved by their fans, creating pop hits that would create mainstream fame across the world.
