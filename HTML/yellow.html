Yellow Submarine

January 17, 1969

“At the start, all four of us hoped for something more classic like Pinocchio or Snow White. I felt it lacked the ingenuity and the warmth and overall magic you associate with Disney. The end result was that the Yellow Submarine just didn’t draw me into it. Basically, I thought it was a lot of very clever sequences but nothing more” - Paul McCartney

Under contract with United Artists to make a fourth album, the band settled on an animated film with actors voices and Beatles music. “Yellow Submarine” written by McCartney and orignally on Revolver was always meant as a childrens song. “Hey Bulldog” goes back to the R&B riffs of earlier beatles work. The cover art of the Album was the same cartoon flyers to promote the movie. In the end the album was very little regarded by the band and more of a contractual obligation.
