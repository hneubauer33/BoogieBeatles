Beatles For Sale

December 4, 1964

‘I’m A Loser’ is me in my Dylan period, because the word ‘clown’ is in it. I objected to the word ‘clown’, because that was always artsy-fartsy, but Dylan had used it so I thought it was all right, and it rhymed with whatever I was doing.” - John Lennon

Recorded during the height of their fame Beatles for Sale was the group’s fourth album released in under two years. The album contained six covers and eight new tracks. After two full years of recorded and performing, the bands music began to take more somber themes like in “I’m a Loser” and “No Reply.” This was the first album in which the band attended mixing sessions. The art cover was photographed again by Robert Freedman, and in The Beatles Style they all wore the same black turtle neck attire.
