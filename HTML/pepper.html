Sgt. Peppers
Lonely Hearts Club Band

May 26, 1967

“The mood of the album was in the spirit of the age, because we ourselves were fitting into the mood of the time. The idea wasn’t to do anything to cater for that mood – we happened to be in that mood anyway.” - Paul McCartney

Sgt. Pepper’s Lonely Hearts Club Band caused an exposive shift in popular music, embodying the ‘Summer of Love’ of 1967. Spending over 400 hours in the studio, The Beatles created sounds that had never been heard before such as the carnival sounds in “For The Benefit of Mister Kite” and the animal stampede in “Good Morning, Good Morning.” Viewing themselves as artists rather than performers the Band brought on a new identity, creating an album embodying a Rock Opera. The band stands amongst cardboard cutouts of guru’s and celebrities on the cover, taking on their new persona. “A Day in the Life” remains one of the most revered songs of all time.
