Abbey Road

September 26, 1969

“The Beatles really could play music together when they weren’t uptight. And if I get a thing going, Ringo knows where to go. Like that. We’ve played together so long that it fits. That’s the only thing I sometimes miss is, is being able to just sort of blink or make a certain noise and I know they’ll all know where we’re going on an ad lib thing.” - John Lennon

Unknown to the band, Abbey Road would be their last recorded album. Named after the road they took to get to EMI studios, the cover art had not title nor the bands name on it. While their Apples records business was in turmoil and bickerings amongst the group increased, while in the studio their bond seemed strong. Songs like “Carry That Weight” and “She’s So Heavy” were about their failing business and the stress it put on the band.
