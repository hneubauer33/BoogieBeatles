The Beatles (The White Album)

November 22, 1968

“I think it was a very good album. It stood up, but it wasn’t a pleasant one to make. Then again, sometimes those things work for your art. The fact that it’s got so much on it is one of the things that’s cool about it. The songs are very varied. I think it’s a fine album.” - Paul McCartney

In response to the astounding success of Sgt. Pepper and failure of Magical Mystery Tour television film, not to mention drug abuse and relationship turblance, The Beatles focused on creating a simple 30 song self titled album. Much of the album was written while traveling in India such as “Sexy Sadie” and “Dear Prudence.” During the recording of the album the band found themselves the least united. Individualism shines through on the album, for instance Lennon’s “Julia” is a solo song about his late mother and “Why Don’t We Do it in the Road” was just McCartney and Starr.
