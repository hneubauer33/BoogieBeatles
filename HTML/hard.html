A Hard Day's Night

July 10, 1964

“We were different. We were older. We knew each other on all kinds of levels that we didn’t when we were teenagers. The early stuff – the Hard Day’s Night period, I call it – was the sexual equivalent of the beginning hysteria of a relationship.” - John Lennon

The title, coined by Ringo, was released in tangent with their first film. The film was critically acclaimed and 40 years later Times magazine named it as one of the 100 all time great films. Hard Day’s Night was also the first album that consisted of completely original songs. During this time period, the Beatles were constantly busy with tours and tv show appearances. That did not stop them from creating songs that would change pop music forever such as “Tell Me Why” and “Can’t Buy me Love.” The opening chord to A Hard Day’s Night remains iconic.
